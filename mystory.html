Lifestyle changes

When I returned from China in 2017, I realized I needed a sport to maintain my fitness and healthy habits. Running is easily one of the most accessibles sports on the planet! Grab a pair of shoes and run! As I got more competitive, I invested in a pair of shoes that matched the needs of my feet, and since then have been updating them every 300 miles. My mother died of complications from diabetes, so in the last few years I've been paying close attention to my A1C. Realizing that making lifestyle changes later in life would be more challenging than making them now, I started following the "Mediterranean Diet": eliminating all meat except for seafood, and maintaining a strong intake of seeds, as well as olive oil.

This proved to be successful in lowering my A1C! I am in the lowest bracket of the "average" range; meaning I have an even lower percentage of developing diabets!
